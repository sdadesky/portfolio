Motivation

"Software Is Eating The World" (Mark Andreesen)

Well, that's never been truer. And I'm famished...

I've got more than 30 years of experience in software and applying software to business ventures. From CEO, to CFO, to lead programmer, I've been involved in software integration in the fields of travel,online commerce and finance.

Besides working for mature, well establihed companies, I've managed and launched a number of startup ventures (Getaway Travel International, Akulink Corp, TravelJungle, TradeJolt). Some were very successful, others were failures.

What I've learned is this: careful elaboration of goals and advanced planning are essential, but insufficient. It's said that 90% of software projects come in over budget and behind schedule. That's a horrible track record, but it's probably true.

To combat that you've got to launch even before you're quite ready. You've got to keep your teams small, but duplicate them. Competition can be healthy. Be open-minded. Test often. Fail fast. Always backup! And backup your backups! Managing software versions is tough, managing programmer egos is even tougher. You want to give reign to curiosity and new thinking without fostering chaos. Make meetings with clients or coworkers frequent, but keep them very short and to the point.

I excel at bringing those multiple forces into harmony. I have deep experience at the code level, but also years in project management. I enjoy both activities and am comfortable in both roles.I love working with systems, but enjoy working with people.

My management motto?

Thinking out of the box is great, but make damn sure the box stays on the horizon...
